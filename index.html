Learn and practice verb conjugations in Spanish

Indicate an action

A verb is a word that indicates someone is 'doing' or ''being'. It is an action word

A 'doing' word describs an action.

Matt runs three times per week.

Infinitive = Basic

The inifinitive is the basic form of the verb. The infinitive will only tell you what action is occuring.

The conjugation is the change made to indicate who done what, and when.

Infinitive

Dictionary verbs are infinitive

Verbs in the dictionary are always in the infinitive.

Spanish verbs in the infinitive always end in either -AR, -ER or -IR,

Hablar. Comer. Venir.

Infinitves are used when following other verbs

When using two verbs together, the second verb is usually infinitive.

I want to run today

Quiero correr hoy
